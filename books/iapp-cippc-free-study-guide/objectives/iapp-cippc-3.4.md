# 3.4: Understand consent expectations for collection, use, and disclosure

Source: IAPP CIPP/C Free Study Guide

## Source limitation

The IAPP CIPP/C Free Study Guide is an introductory preparation document that
provides exam logistics, certification structure, and high-level domain
outlines. It explicitly notes that substantive legal content requires the
official textbook: "Canadian Privacy: Data Protection Law and Policy for the
Practitioner."

The guide does not contain specific content on consent expectations for public
sector collection, use, and disclosure under the Privacy Act or provincial
FOIPPAs.

## Available content from this source

### Body of knowledge reference

Domain III (Canadian Privacy Laws and Practices - Public-Sector) covers:

- Privacy principles foundational to the Privacy Act
- Privacy Impact Assessments
- Applicability of provincial/territorial Freedom of Information and Protection
  of Privacy Acts

### Key subject matter areas

The guide identifies these public sector topics for the exam:

- Roles and oversight powers of Canadian information and privacy commissioners
- Select public sector information access laws such as the Privacy Act
