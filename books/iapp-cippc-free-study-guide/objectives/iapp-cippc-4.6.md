# 4.6: Understand electronic health record security requirements

Source: IAPP CIPP/C Free Study Guide

## Key concepts

- **Health information privacy statutes**: The study guide identifies Ontario's
  PHIPA, Alberta's HIA, Manitoba's PHIA, and Saskatchewan's HIPA as key health
  sector laws covered on the CIPP/C exam
  - These statutes govern electronic health records in their respective
    jurisdictions
  - Security requirements are embedded within each provincial health privacy
    law

- **Body of knowledge coverage**: Domain IV covers "Canadian Privacy Laws and
  Practices - Health Sector" with the objective to "Understand when to apply
  the various health privacy acts of the provinces and territories"

## Limitations of this source

The IAPP Free Study Guide is an exam preparation overview document that:

- Lists topic areas without detailed explanations
- References the authoritative textbook "Canadian Privacy: Data Protection Law
  and Policy for the Practitioner" for substantive content
- Recommends 30+ hours of study using the official textbook and training
  materials

For detailed coverage of electronic health record security requirements,
consult the CIPP/C textbook or the `iapp-cippc-bok` source which contains the
full body of knowledge content.

## Related topics

- Provincial health privacy acts (PHIPA, HIA, PHIA, HIPA): Each contains
  specific EHR security provisions
- Privacy impact assessments: Required for health information systems in
  several jurisdictions
- Health information custodians: Organizations responsible for EHR security
  compliance
